21.1 SQL

21.1.1 Key concepts of normalization

Normalization is achieved through various “normal forms,” each with specific rules to ensure better database design. The main normal forms are:

First Normal Form (1NF)

The goal of the first normal form is to ensure that each column contains only atomic (indivisible) values, and each row is unique.

Rules

  • Remove duplicate columns: Each column must be unique and represent a single attribute.

  • Ensure each column is atomic: No repeating groups or arrays. For example, you shouldn’t have a column with multiple phone numbers separated by commas.

  • Ensure each row is unique: Use a primary key to uniquely identify each row.

Example

Before 1NF:

student_id name subjects
1 Mary Math, Science
2 Paul Math, English

After 1NF:

student_id name subject
1 Mary Math
1 Mary Science
2 Paul Math
2 Paul English


Second Normal Form (2NF)

The aim of the second normal form is to ensure that all non-key attributes are fully dependent on the primary key.

Rules

  • Be in 1NF: The table must first satisfy all the requirements of 1NF.

  • Remove partial dependencies: Each non-key attribute must depend on the whole primary key, not just part of it. This usually means removing data that doesn’t depend entirely on the primary key into a separate table.

Example

Before 2NF:

order_id product_id product_name quantity
101 1 Laptop 5
102 2 Phone 8

The problem here is that product_name depends only on product_id, not the whole primary key (order_id, product_id).

After 2NF:

Orders Table:

order_id product_id quantity
101 1 5
102 2 8

Product Table:

product_id product_name
1 Laptop
2 Phone


Third Normal Form (3NF)

The Thrid normal form goal is to ensure that all non-key attributes are not only fully dependent on the primary key but are also independent of each other.

Rules

  • Be in 2NF: The table must first satisfy all the requirements of 2NF.

  • Remove transitive dependencies: Non-key attributes should not depend on other non-key attributes.

Example

Before 3NF:

employee_id name department department_head
1 Mary Accounting Tom
2 Paul IT Benjamin

The problem here is that the department_head depends on department, not on employee_id.

After 3NF:

Employee Table:

employee_id name department
1 Mary Accounting
2 Paul IT

Department Table:

department department_head
Accounting Tom
IT Benjamin
When not to normalize too much

While normalization is essential, over-normalization can lead to:

  • Complex queries: Highly normalized tables may require complex joins.
  • Performance hits: Excessive joins can slow down query performance.
